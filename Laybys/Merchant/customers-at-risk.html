Risk signals — what we look for

A customer is flagged as at-risk if they show any of these patterns:

  • 3 or more failed payments within 30 days
  • Direct debit dishonour (account closed, frozen, or invalid)
  • Lay-by overdue 7+ days with no customer response
  • Payment score drops to B or below based on rolling 90-day history
  • Customer has stopped logging in while a lay-by is overdue

Outreach playbook

Highest-recovery sequence for repeat customers:

  1. Day 1 — Friendly check-in email ("just letting you know your payment didn't go through")
  2. Day 3 — SMS with one-tap card update link
  3. Day 5 — Personal email offering hardship terms (extension, smaller instalments)
  4. Day 7 — Phone call from a real person — best for repeat customers, often resolves in 5 minutes
  5. Day 10+ — Mutual cancellation conversation if no resolution path is workable
