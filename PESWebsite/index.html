Most Requested

Phase I Environmental Site Assessment

Identify potential environmental concerns or red flags before you purchase or finance a commercial property. Compliant with ASTM E1527-21 standards.

  • Historical records research & regulatory database review
  • Physical site reconnaissance & adjacent property survey
  • Interviews with owners, occupants, and local officials
  • Recognized Environmental Conditions (RECs) analysis
  • Clear, actionable report with recommendations
Request Phase I Quote →
⏱ Typical delivery: 10 business days
Investigation

Phase II Environmental Site Investigation

When a Phase I identifies potential contamination, Phase II confirms or rules it out through sampling and laboratory analysis.

  • Targeted soil and groundwater sampling
  • Certified laboratory analysis of collected samples
  • Comparison against federal & state cleanup standards
  • Risk assessment and contamination delineation
  • Remediation recommendations if contamination confirmed
Request Phase II Quote →
⏱ Typical delivery: 15–20 business days
Lender Solutions

Transaction Screen & Lender Risk Screening

A streamlined environmental evaluation designed for lenders who need fast risk assessment for commercial loan portfolios.

  • SBA RSRA-equivalent screening
  • Database review and limited site reconnaissance
  • Risk-level categorization (low/moderate/high)
  • Recommendations for further assessment if needed

Gordon S. Cruickshank

President & Founder — PES, Inc. | Since 1996

Gordon founded Property Environmental Services in December 1996 and has since led the firm through 2,000+ environmental assessments for over 150 organizations. His work spans private and public sectors, including developing closure plans for U.S. Air Force and Army bases and crafting hazardous material management protocols for the U.S. Coast Guard.

His industry leadership includes serving as Chairman of Environmental Committees for the National Association of Industrial and Office Properties (NAIOP) and the Suburban Maryland Building Industry Association (SMBIA). He is a contributing member to nationally recognized ASTM standards that govern how environmental site assessments are conducted across the country.
